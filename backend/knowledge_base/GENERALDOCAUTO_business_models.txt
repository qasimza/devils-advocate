BUSINESS MODELS — DEBATE KNOWLEDGE BASE
========================================

SAAS BENCHMARKS
- Healthy SaaS CAC payback period: under 12 months for SMB, under 18 months for mid-market, under 24 months for enterprise.
- LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 is the minimum acceptable benchmark. Below 2:1 = broken model. Above 5:1 = underinvesting in growth.
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) benchmarks: world-class = 130%+, healthy = 110–120%, warning = below 100% (shrinking even without churn).
- Annual churn rate thresholds: SMB SaaS — under 10% is acceptable, under 5% is strong. Enterprise SaaS — under 5% expected, under 2% is excellent.
- Monthly churn of 2% compounds to 22% annual churn — a company losing 22% of its base every year cannot grow without massive new acquisition spend.
- Median SaaS gross margin is 72% (Bessemer, 2023). Below 60% indicates infrastructure or COGS problems that compress fundraising valuation multiples.
- Rule of 40: Revenue growth % + profit margin % should exceed 40 for a healthy SaaS company. Most early-stage companies sacrifice margin for growth.

MARKETPLACE UNIT ECONOMICS
- Cold start problem: a marketplace with fewer than a threshold number of suppliers is worthless to buyers, and vice versa. Getting both sides to threshold simultaneously is the hardest problem in startups.
- Uber spent $2B in subsidies in 2015–2016 to achieve liquidity in new markets. Most startups cannot subsidize both sides.
- Take rate benchmarks: gig economy 15–30%, B2B procurement 2–5%, financial products 0.5–2%, real estate 2–5%.
- Sustainable marketplace take rates are constrained by the supplier's ability to disintermediate. A platform charging 30%+ invites suppliers to build direct channels.
- OpenTable charged restaurants $1/diner for years. When it raised rates, restaurants revolted and shifted to Resy. Take rates are rarely durable above 15% in competitive two-sided markets.
- Liquidity threshold: Airbnb found that markets needed 300+ listings per major city before organic search-driven demand became self-sustaining.

D2C BENCHMARKS AND FAILURE MODES
- D2C CAC on Meta averaged ~$87 in 2023, up from ~$9 in 2013 (10x increase in a decade).
- iOS 14 ATT update (April 2021) increased reported CPAs for D2C brands by 30–60% within 90 days.
- D2C LTV benchmark: healthy LTV is 3× first-order AOV. For a $60 AOV brand, LTV should be $180+ to sustain a $60–80 CAC.
- Return rates by category: apparel 20–30%, electronics 15–20%, footwear 25–35%. High return rates collapse gross margin in D2C.
- Brandless raised $240M, targeted CPG disruption at scale, shut down in 2020. Core problem: no brand differentiation + high fulfillment costs eliminated margin.
- Casper IPO'd in 2020 at $575M valuation (down from $1.1B private). CAC was ~$300 per mattress while AOV was ~$1,000 — technically viable but insufficient for a high-volume business.

FREEMIUM MODEL BENCHMARKS
- Industry average freemium-to-paid conversion: 2–5%.
- Spotify: ~26% conversion (exceptional — driven by podcast/exclusive content and premium lock-in features).
- Dropbox: ~4% conversion at scale. Survived because viral free tier drove organic growth offsetting low conversion.
- Freemium fails when: free tier is too generous (why pay?), free tier is too restricted (no viral loop), or infrastructure costs per free user are too high.
- Slack converted ~30% of free teams to paid — but required enterprise features and admin controls unavailable in free tier to drive conversion.

SUBSCRIPTION CHURN MATH
- At 3% monthly churn: median customer lifetime = 33 months. At 5% monthly churn: median lifetime = 20 months.
- A subscription business with 5% monthly churn needs to replace 46% of its customer base every year just to stay flat.
- Consumer subscription churn is structurally higher than B2B: Netflix US monthly churn ~2%, median consumer app subscription churn ~5–8%.
- Annual prepay reduces churn by locking customers in, but masks retention problems — churn spikes at renewal are often invisible until year 2.

HARDWARE BUSINESS MODEL TRAPS
- Hardware gross margins average 30–50% vs SaaS at 70–80%. This compression makes CAC payback periods 2–3× harder to achieve.
- Razor/blade models (cheap hardware, recurring consumable revenue) require sufficient installed base before consumable revenue becomes meaningful — typically takes 3–5 years.
- Peloton: hardware margin ~43%, but subscription attach rate dropped from 92% to 67% by 2022, collapsing the model's unit economics.
- Juicero: $120M raised, $400 hardware, $7–8/pack consumables. Consumers discovered they could squeeze packs by hand. The hardware provided no durable value-add.

SERVICES-DISGUISED-AS-SOFTWARE
- "Tech-enabled services" companies trade at 1–3× revenue. Pure SaaS companies trade at 5–15× revenue.
- If removing the software layer leaves a functioning services business, investors will price it as a services business.
- Compass raised $1.5B claiming to be a tech company. Traded at services-company multiples after IPO. Stock dropped 90% from peak.
